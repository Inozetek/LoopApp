Privacy Policy

Loop — Last updated: February 22, 2026

Loop ("we," "us," or "our") is developed and operated by Nick Casey. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use the Loop mobile application and related services (collectively, the "Service"). Please read this policy carefully. By using Loop, you agree to the practices described herein.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Account Information: When you create an account, we collect your name, email address, and password (or authentication tokens if you sign in via Google or Apple).
  • Profile Information: Interests, activity preferences, budget preferences, preferred times of day, home address, and work address that you provide during onboarding or later in your settings.
  • Calendar Data: If you choose to connect your Google Calendar or Apple Calendar, we access your calendar events (titles, times, and locations) to identify free time slots and provide timely recommendations.
  • Feedback & Reviews: Thumbs-up/thumbs-down ratings, feedback tags, written notes, and comments you submit about activities.
  • Social Data: Friend connections, group plan details, messages sent within group chats, and RSVP responses.
  • Payment Information: If you subscribe to Loop Plus or operate a business account, payment is processed by Stripe. We do not store your full credit card number. We retain only Stripe customer IDs and subscription metadata.
  • Communications: If you contact us for support, we collect the content of your messages and any attachments.

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Location Data: With your permission, we collect your device's GPS coordinates. We use "when-in-use" location access to provide distance-based recommendations. If you opt in to commute detection, we use "always-on" location access to learn your commute route and suggest on-route activities. See Section 3 for details.
  • Device Information: Device model, operating system version, unique device identifiers, and push notification tokens.
  • Usage Analytics: We use PostHog to collect anonymized usage data such as screens viewed, features used, session duration, and interaction patterns. This data is not linked to your personal identity.
  • Crash Reports: We use Sentry to collect crash logs and error reports to diagnose and fix bugs. These reports may contain device state, stack traces, and anonymized session context.

1.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Google Places API: We retrieve activity and venue data (names, addresses, ratings, photos, opening hours, reviews) from the Google Places API to populate recommendations. Google's own privacy practices govern data collected by Google.
  • Authentication Providers: If you sign in with Google or Apple, we receive your name, email, and profile picture as authorized by you during sign-in.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide Personalized Recommendations: Match you with activities based on your interests, location, calendar availability, feedback history, and contextual signals (time of day, weather).
  • Operate the Service: Create and manage your account, process subscriptions, display your calendar, and enable social features (friends, group planning, messaging).
  • Improve the Algorithm: Use your feedback (thumbs up/down, tags) to continuously improve recommendation quality. Our AI learning system updates your preference profile over time.
  • Facilitate Group Planning: Calculate geographic midpoints, find overlapping free times among friends, and suggest group-friendly activities.
  • Send Notifications: Deliver push notifications about recommendations, departure alerts, friend requests, group invitations, and feedback reminders (subject to your notification preferences).
  • Process Payments: Handle subscription billing for Loop Plus users and business accounts via Stripe.
  • Ensure Security: Detect fraud, enforce rate limits, and protect against unauthorized access.
  • Analyze and Improve: Use anonymized analytics (PostHog) and crash reports (Sentry) to understand usage patterns, fix bugs, and improve the Service.
  • Provide Business Analytics: Deliver aggregated, anonymized performance metrics (impressions, click-through rates, conversion counts) to business account holders. Individual user data is never shared with businesses.

3. Location Data Important

Location data is central to Loop's recommendations. Here is how we handle it:

3.1 When-in-Use Location

When you have the app open, we use your current GPS coordinates to calculate distances to recommended activities and provide location-relevant suggestions. This requires "when-in-use" location permission on your device.

3.2 Always-On Location (Optional)

You may optionally enable "always-on" location access for commute detection. This allows Loop to learn your typical home-to-work route and suggest activities along your commute. You can disable this at any time in your device settings or within Loop's settings screen.

3.3 Smart Scheduling (Future Feature)

In a future release, Loop may offer a Smart Scheduling feature that uses background location tracking to recognize recurring places (e.g., gym, office) and automatically populate your calendar. This feature will be strictly opt-in with clear explanations before activation. Location history older than 30 days will be automatically deleted.

3.4 Location Data Safeguards

  • We do not sell location data to any third party.
  • Raw GPS coordinates are never shared with business accounts.
  • You can disable location access at any time through your device's system settings.
  • You can delete all stored location data from your account settings.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We share data only in the following limited circumstances:

  • With Your Consent: When you choose to share your Loop (daily schedule) with friends or participate in group plans, selected information is visible to those users according to your privacy settings.
  • Aggregated Business Analytics: Business account holders receive aggregated, anonymized metrics about how their listings perform (e.g., total impressions, click counts). These reports never contain individual user identities, locations, or personal details.
  • Service Providers: We share data with the third-party services listed in Section 4 strictly to operate the Service. These providers are contractually obligated to protect your data.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, court order, or government request, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of Loop, our users, or the public.
  • Business Transfer: If Loop is acquired, merged, or undergoes a change of control, user data may be transferred to the successor entity. We will notify you before your data becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

6. Data Retention

  • Account Data: Retained for as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, all associated personal data is deleted within 30 days, except where retention is required by law.
  • Feedback & Ratings: Retained to improve recommendations for as long as your account exists. Deleted upon account deletion.
  • Location History: If Smart Scheduling is enabled, raw location data older than 30 days is automatically deleted. Commute route data (aggregated polyline) is retained until you disable the feature or delete your account.
  • Analytics Data (PostHog): Anonymized analytics data is retained for up to 24 months. Because it is anonymized, it cannot be linked back to you after account deletion.
  • Crash Reports (Sentry): Retained for 90 days for debugging purposes.
  • Payment Records: Transaction records are retained as required by tax and financial regulations (typically 7 years).
  • Calendar Data: Synced calendar events are stored for as long as your account is active and your calendar remains connected. You can disconnect your calendar at any time.

7. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data:

  • All data in transit is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS.
  • Database access is protected by Supabase's Row-Level Security (RLS) policies, ensuring users can only access their own data.
  • Authentication tokens (JWT) use short expiry times and are refreshed automatically.
  • API endpoints are protected by rate limiting to prevent abuse.
  • Payment data is handled entirely by Stripe (PCI DSS Level 1 certified). We never store full credit card numbers.
  • Access to production infrastructure is restricted to authorized personnel only.

While we strive to protect your information, no method of electronic storage or transmission is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

9.4 Right to Non-Discrimination
